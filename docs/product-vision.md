# Nudge Product Vision

Nudge is a private daily operating layer for modern life. It helps a person notice what changed, remember what matters, decide what deserves attention, and act at the right time without surrendering control.

The name comes from the Roman Nudge: household guardian spirits associated with protection, continuity, memory, and the everyday life of a home. That is the product posture: not a generic chatbot, not a sales CRM, and not an autonomous operator, but a trusted guardian for the user's commitments, relationships, reflections, and routines.

## Promise

Nudge turns scattered personal signals into a calm Daily Operating Loop:

```txt
signals -> memory -> understanding -> decision -> action -> feedback -> adaptation
```

Each day, Nudge should answer:

- What changed?
- What did I miss?
- Who or what needs attention?
- What should I do next?
- Why is this suggestion grounded in evidence?

## Product Feel

Nudge should feel private, protective, and quietly competent. It should feel more like a household intelligence or personal command sanctuary than a generic productivity dashboard.

Design cues:

- warm, calm, trustworthy
- source-linked and inspectable
- action-oriented but never pushy
- emotionally aware without being sentimental
- timeless rather than trend-coded

Visual directions:

- hearth, doorway, guardian, small flame, arch, shrine, or protective circle
- warm ivory, terracotta, muted gold, black olive, stone gray
- modern serif or humanist wordmark with subtle Roman/architectural influence

## Core Product Concepts

- **Daily Operating Loop**: the primary product loop for Digest, focus, Action Points, reflection, feedback, and adaptation.
- **Digest**: a short summary of missed or new information since the last loop.
- **Action Point**: a concrete suggested next step grounded in signals, memory, goals, commitments, or Relationship Memory.
- **Relationship Memory**: the memory domain for people, important dates, preferences, context, and follow-ups.
- **Review Queue**: the Human-in-the-Loop surface where proposals wait for accept/edit/reject decisions.
- **Resume Token**: the continuation context that lets agents/workflows pause for approval and continue afterward.
- **Consent Grant**: an explicit permission record for sharing, redaction, duration, and revocation.

## Trust Principles

- Private by default.
- Read-first, draft-first.
- Human-in-the-Loop for sensitive memory, external writes, Consent Grants, and behavior-changing automation.
- Source-linked memories and recommendations.
- Wide Logs and traces for operational visibility without raw personal payload dumps.
- Per-user evolution before global templates.
- Golden-case evaluation before activating evolved routines.

## Near-Term Wedge

The current wedge is the Daily Operating Loop through notes, memory, review, and closure:

1. Daily note and journal capture.
2. Revision tracking and source-linked memory documents.
3. Draft extraction of actions, reminders, events, questions, ideas, and memory candidates.
4. Reviewable Proposals that can become Commitments.
5. Outcomes that close the feedback loop.
6. Conversation sessions that can retrieve memory and draft loop operations.
7. Private data export and account deletion.
8. Golden-case evals for agent/product behavior.

## Long-Term Vision

Nudge becomes the user's private adaptive memory and decision layer across life domains: work, relationships, calendar, commitments, routines, health, learning, and long-term goals. It earns more agency over time through evaluation, trust, and explicit consent, but it never silently becomes the owner of the user's life.
