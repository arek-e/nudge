# Lares Product Vision

Lares is a private daily operating layer for modern life. It helps a person notice what changed, remember what matters, decide what deserves attention, and act at the right time without surrendering control.

The name comes from the Roman Lares: household guardian spirits associated with protection, continuity, memory, and the everyday life of a home. That is the product posture: not a generic chatbot, not a sales CRM, and not an autonomous operator, but a trusted guardian for the user's commitments, relationships, reflections, and routines.

## Promise

Lares turns scattered personal signals into a calm Daily Operating Loop:

```txt
signals -> memory -> understanding -> decision -> action -> feedback -> adaptation
```

Each day, Lares should answer:

- What changed?
- What did I miss?
- Who or what needs attention?
- What should I do next?
- Why is this suggestion grounded in evidence?

## Product Feel

Lares should feel private, protective, and quietly competent. It should feel more like a household intelligence or personal command sanctuary than a generic productivity dashboard.

Design cues:

- warm, calm, trustworthy
- source-linked and inspectable
- action-oriented but never pushy
- emotionally aware without being sentimental
- timeless rather than trend-coded

Visual directions:

- hearth, doorway, guardian, small flame, arch, shrine, or protective circle
- warm ivory, terracotta, muted gold, black olive, stone gray
- modern serif or humanist wordmark with subtle Roman/architectural influence

## Core Product Concepts

- **Daily Operating Loop**: the primary product loop for Digest, focus, Action Points, reflection, feedback, and adaptation.
- **Digest**: a short summary of missed or new information since the last loop.
- **Action Point**: a concrete suggested next step grounded in signals, memory, goals, commitments, or Relationship Memory.
- **Relationship Memory**: the memory domain for people, important dates, preferences, context, and follow-ups.
- **Review Queue**: the Human-in-the-Loop surface where proposals wait for accept/edit/reject decisions.
- **Resume Token**: the continuation context that lets agents/workflows pause for approval and continue afterward.
- **Consent Grant**: an explicit permission record for sharing, redaction, duration, and revocation.

## Trust Principles

- Private by default.
- Read-first, draft-first.
- Human-in-the-Loop for sensitive memory, external writes, Consent Grants, and behavior-changing automation.
- Source-linked memories and recommendations.
- Wide Logs and traces for operational visibility without raw personal payload dumps.
- Per-user evolution before global templates.
- Golden-case evaluation before activating evolved routines.

## Near-Term Wedge

The first useful product is the Daily Operating Loop:

1. Manual morning check-in.
2. Read-only calendar context.
3. Manually entered Relationship Memory.
4. Digest of missed/new information.
5. Source-linked Action Points.
6. Review Queue for proposed memories and actions.
7. Evening reflection and feedback.
8. Golden-case evals for Digest improvements.

## Long-Term Vision

Lares becomes the user's private adaptive memory and decision layer across life domains: work, relationships, calendar, commitments, routines, health, learning, and long-term goals. It earns more agency over time through evaluation, trust, and explicit consent, but it never silently becomes the owner of the user's life.
